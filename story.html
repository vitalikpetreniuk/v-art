we boost digital art market

Our story

We launched V-Art with an idea of a transparent and legal infrastructure for digital art in March 2020. With this idea, we inspired contemporary Ukrainian artist Roman Minin, who became co-founder and ambassador of V-Art. We were looking for technical team who would be able to realize our ambitious goals to digitalize the art market for a long time, and finally we found partners who had broad experience in implementing projects in the ArtTech and AR field.

“Aha“ moment came to us with changes that happened in the world due to pandemic:

art came over to virtual reality, and the NFT mechanism has made secure ownership of a digital asset possible.

  • NFT + for digital  & digitalized art
  • Index of value and provenance tracking
  • Easy-to-demonstrate virtual galleries & “pocket” collections
Platform to exhibit, sell and collect digital art
  • E-certification & system of licenses
  • Pre-platform & IP
    protection
  • Networkong & tools for community building

Team

Our team combines expertise and background in the field of art,
IP protection, IT and Art & Finance.
Anastasia Gliebova
CEO

5+ years in creative management. Curator of the Art Business School, Head of Research & Partner in ILTI.

More than 15 years I dealing in field of intellectual property and creative industries rights. More than 50 projects, startups and businesses behind. We served them in ILTI, which I head, providing various services, starting from conflict resolution, ending with comprehensive support of business processes and the formation of a “turnkey” IP profile.

Every day I faced problems of creative people, their ignorance/newness of legal issues, their problems with dealers, promoters, gallery owners and other intermediaries who did not prescribe the framework of cooperation in the contract, did not adhere to oral agreements and created unconscionability for artists. There were also opposite situations when the artist allowed themselves to cheat on a professional mediator or art manager, not to adhere to the agreement.

Despite mentioned problems, there is the main one – Ukrainian art market is local, it doesn’t have necessary potential for scaling the artist, his access to a global audience. Over the years of practice, I have developed an understanding how to integrate Ukrainian into a global context, to develop the secondary market, to form a culture of intellectual property and understanding the value of a creative product.

The development of technologies and digitalization processes have forced to look towards the automation of many processes that would facilitate the artist's work. My ideal is a democratic self-regulated market with the proper rules, where the cooperation of the gallery and the artist is a win-win/mutually beneficial process of moving towards, not establishing a monopoly on creativity.

I explained my ideas to Roman Minin, one of the pioneers of digital art in Ukraine. As it turned out, he also had a similar idea. This way, at one moment the V-Art project was born, the name of which, we also came up with together. After that there were days, weeks and months of teamwork, brainstorming, until the startup V-Art appeared, with already functioning application for virtual exhibitions. The next stage- marketplace built on a combination of unique technologies and legal tools.
